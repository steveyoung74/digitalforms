What is a Politically Exposed Person (PEP)?

A PEP is someone who holds (or has held within the last 12 months) a prominent public function, including:

  • Heads of state, government, ministers and deputy ministers
  • Members of parliament or similar legislative bodies
  • Members of governing bodies of political parties
  • Members of supreme courts or constitutional courts
  • Members of boards of central banks
  • Ambassadors, high commissioners, and senior military officers
  • Directors and board members of state-owned enterprises

Family members and close associates of PEPs are also considered to be PEPs for regulatory purposes.
